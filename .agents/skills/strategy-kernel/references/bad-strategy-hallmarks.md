# Bad Strategy Hallmarks

Bad strategy arises from misconceptions, leadership dysfunctions, and avoidance of the hard work of making choices. These four hallmarks signal bad strategy — stop and challenge immediately when detected.

## 1. Fluff

- **What it is:** Abstract jargon or superficial slogans masquerading as strategic concepts. Hides the absence of substance with unnecessary complexity.
- **How to detect:** Strip away the fancy words. If nothing concrete remains, it is fluff. Look for buzzwords doing the work that analysis should do.
- **How to challenge:** "What does this actually mean in concrete terms? What specific obstacle does it address?"

## 2. Failure to Face the Challenge

- **What it is:** Strategy that never identifies the actual obstacles. No explicit diagnosis of the fundamental problem. Jumps straight to actions or goals.
- **How to detect:** Ask "what is the challenge?" If the answer is vague, circular, or absent, the challenge has not been faced.
- **How to challenge:** "What is the specific obstacle preventing progress? Without defining the challenge, it is impossible to assess whether the strategy addresses it."

## 3. Mistaking Goals for Strategy

- **What it is:** Statements of desire, visions, or ambitious targets presented as strategy. Goals are not a plan for overcoming obstacles. This represents active avoidance of the hard work of strategy.
- **How to detect:** Check if there is a method. "Be number one in the market" states a destination but says nothing about the route.
- **How to challenge:** "That is what you want to achieve — but how will you get there? What method addresses the obstacles in your way?"

## 4. Bad Strategic Objectives

Two distinct forms:

### "Dog's Dinner" Objectives

- **What it is:** A messy, jumbled list of things to achieve that scatters energy and resources without focusing on critical issues. Ignores cognitive constraints — leadership can only concentrate on a few critical issues at a time.
- **How to detect:** Count the objectives. If there are more than 3-4 with no clear priority or coherence, it is a Dog's Dinner.
- **How to challenge:** "Which ONE of these matters most right now? Good strategy concentrates resources on the critical issue, not spreads them across everything."

### "Blue Sky" Objectives

- **What it is:** A simple restatement of the desired end state, ignoring the difficulty of getting there. The objective is as hard as the original challenge. The leader has not absorbed the complexity to deliver a solvable problem.
- **How to detect:** Compare the objective to the original challenge. If achieving the objective requires solving the entire challenge, it adds no strategic value.
- **How to challenge:** "This objective is as hard as the original challenge. What feasible step gets you closer without requiring you to solve the whole problem at once?"
