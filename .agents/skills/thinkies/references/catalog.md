# Kent Beck's Thinkies Catalog

All Thinkies in this catalog were created and curated by Kent Beck (https://kentbeck.com/).

## General Principles

- A Thinkie is a habit of thought that generates an idea given some context
- Each Thinkie consists of a **Pattern** (situation to recognize) and a **Transformation** (action to apply)
- The process: Match the pattern, apply the transformation, then evaluate results
- Focus on *generating ideas*, not necessarily *good ideas*
- Many applications won't yield useful results—don't be discouraged
- Separate idea generation from judgment; pre-judging prevents effectiveness
- Thinkies help shift perspectives from "local minima" and unstick thinking

## Categories

**Language-oriented Thinkies:** Manipulating syntax and words being used
**Project Management Thinkies:** Related to how projects are managed and seen
**Models:** Using mental models or analogies to view situations
**Frames:** Re-framing troublesome situations

## The Thinkies

### 1. "Obviously..."
**Pattern:** Someone begins a sentence with, "Obviously…"
**Transformation:** Ask what is true if they are wrong. What happens if you make the opposite assumption?
**Detail:** Language-oriented Thinkie. Encourages questioning assumptions, even if the "obvious" statement turns out to be true.

### 2. Break Dependency
**Pattern:** Your project can't progress because you're waiting on another project/team/organization/company
**Transformation:** Break the dependency. What would happen if you did just enough work yourself?
**Detail:** Elaborate dependency structures built for "efficiency" can hinder progress.

### 3. Can't/Because → When/Can
**Pattern:** Statements containing "can't" followed by "because"
**Transformation:** Reframe as "When [condition], we can [action]"
**Detail:** Transforms limitations into conditional possibilities. Example: "We can't release daily because too many bugs" becomes "When we have fewer bugs, we can release daily."

### 4. Customer's Voice
**Pattern:** The team is being tugged in so many directions by so many people that they can't make progress on anything
**Transformation:** Have the customer speak with one voice
**Detail:** Conceived as inverse to Direct Feedback.

### 5. Dependency Merging
**Pattern:** Progress is blocked by dependency on another project/team/organization/company
**Transformation:** Merge, temporarily or permanently, whole or in part, the two entities
**Detail:** Sometimes blocking dependency suggests organizational structure is incorrect.

### 6. Direct Feedback
**Pattern:** Feedback between those who make changes becomes filtered or aggregated
**Transformation:** Restore direct feedback loops
**Detail:** As projects grow, feedback becomes summarized and filtered, losing important information.

### 7. Half in Half
**Pattern:** Someone gives you a date for a scope
**Transformation:** Ask "Which half of it can you give me in half the time?"
**Detail:** Beck's favorite Thinkie. Helps unstick projects by focusing on priorities and what's truly necessary. Can be applied fractally, down to typing sentences.

### 8. Laughter
**Pattern:** A statement in conversation is followed by nervous laughter
**Transformation:** Take the statement seriously
**Detail:** Language-related Thinkie. Inspired by Shakespeare: "Many a true word hath been spoken in jest."

### 9. Personal Feedback
**Pattern:** Teams not experiencing their own product
**Transformation:** Make feedback personal—use your own product
**Detail:** Example: Payroll company using own system internally provides valuable feedback.

### 10. Project Buffer First
**Pattern:** Starting to plan a project with fixed delivery date
**Transformation:** Start by saying "How certain do we need to be? We'll start with 15% (or 10% or 20%) project buffer"
**Detail:** Unsettling to people because traditional planning involves over-promising and under-delivering.

### 11. Reverse Phrase
**Pattern:** A phrase with 2 symmetric parts
**Transformation:** Reverse the phrase structure to generate new ideas
**Example:** "Engineering turns ideas into reality" → "Science turns reality into ideas"
**Detail:** Most of the time, this won't produce useful results.

### 12. Serialize
**Pattern:** The team is doing things in parallel
**Transformation:** Do them serially instead
**Detail:** Addresses dysfunctional assumptions about projects, possibly from Taylorism.

### 13. Shift Left & Right
**Pattern:** Multi-stage process with handoffs you'd like to eliminate
**Transformation:** Look at value realized by person handed off to. What would it take for someone else to realize that value earlier or later?
**Example:** Classic relationship between developers and testers.

### 14. 1, 10, 100
**Pattern:** Progress blocked by big, scary worry
**Transformation:** Quantify order of magnitude. "Is it $1000? $10,000? $100,000?"
**Detail:** Choose magnitudes so largest suggested number remains manageable.

### 15. Compared to What? (CTW?)
**Pattern:** You hear a judgment without context
**Transformation:** Ask "Compared to what?"
**Detail:** Highly profitable Thinkie. Unsticks brain by challenging vague judgments. From Jerry Weinberg.

### 16. Contradiction
**Pattern:** Faced with big, complicated situation
**Transformation:** Look for contradictions in the situation
**Detail:** Consulting Thinkie for quickly making sense of large amounts of information.

### 17. Defer Decision
**Pattern:** Decision being made without all relevant information or full team buy-in
**Transformation:** Don't decide today. Ask "What if we just didn't decide for a while?"
**Detail:** One of three decision-related Thinkies that can contradict each other.

### 18. Else?
**Pattern:** You have a situation where condition has consequence
**Transformation:** What happens if the condition does not hold?
**Example:** In TCR, what happens if you don't always get back to green tests?

### 19. End To End
**Pattern:** Large, complicated, multi-layered project structured layer-by-layer for "efficiency"
**Transformation:** Structure the project to go end-to-end for tiny subset of possible inputs instead
**Example:** Payroll system consulting with over 100 people involved.

### 20. Envelope Calculation
**Pattern:** You see some data. Do they matter?
**Transformation:** Do the math to put numbers into context
**Detail:** Not complex calculations. If spreadsheet needed, likely too much investment initially.

### 21. Force Decision
**Pattern:** Decision needs to be made but nobody is making it for no good reason
**Transformation:** Ask "If we had to decide today, what would we...?"
**Detail:** First of three decision-related Thinkies, intentionally contradictory.

### 22. Grow Then Split
**Pattern:** Creating something that will have many elements, stuck on where to start
**Transformation:** Start with one tiny element. Grow it. When uncomfortably large, split along revealed natural fault lines
**Detail:** Contrasts with inclination to preemptively split components.

### 23. Half Wavelength Shift
**Pattern:** Multi-stage process A→B→C→D
**Transformation:** What happens if you start with B, C, or D?
**Example:** TDD, where testing precedes coding instead of traditional code-then-test.

### 24. Just Apply
**Pattern:** Faced with confusing situation, don't know what to do next
**Transformation:** Take a model, analogy, or frame and just apply it
**Detail:** Apply without concern for whether model "makes sense."

### 25. Latency, Throughput, & Variance
**Pattern:** Someone says "I need you to go faster!" You're going as fast as you can
**Transformation:** Ask "latency or throughput?" Bias towards reducing variance first
**Detail:** Based on Kingman's Formula. Reducing variance "magically" improves responsiveness.

### 26. Match the Pipes
**Pattern:** Insufficient value flowing through series of steps
**Transformation:** Match capacity of steps like matching pipe diameters
**Detail:** Systems thinking Thinkie. Addresses tendency to get lost in details.

### 27. McDonald's
**Pattern:** Group ready to make decision but is stalling
**Transformation:** Propose a bad-but-not-ludicrous alternative
**Detail:** Named after suggesting "McDonald's" to force group to pick restaurant.

### 28. Measure Later
**Pattern:** Stuck trying to improve multi-step process
**Transformation:** What could you measure later in the process?
**Detail:** Addresses tendency to measure what is easy rather than important.

### 29. Measure Sooner
**Pattern:** Measuring end of multi-stage process but measurement doesn't guide change
**Transformation:** Measure something correlated to success sooner in process
**Example:** If production error rates aren't helpful, measure earlier.

### 30. Measure What Matters
**Pattern:** Measuring steps of multi-stage process but not seeing desired improvement
**Transformation:** Measure the value you actually want to change, even if vague or complicated
**Example:** If profit is goal, measure profit. If customer satisfaction is goal, measure that.

### 31. Parallel Decision
**Pattern:** Must decide between 2 alternatives, decision unclear, stakes high
**Transformation:** Ask "Why not both?"
**Detail:** Final decision Thinkie, contradicting Force Decision and Defer Decision. From Ward Cunningham.

### 32. Rate-Limiting Resource
**Pattern:** Product growing fast but nervous about limits of growth
**Transformation:** What resource will you run out of next? How to increase supply and decrease demand?
**Detail:** Applies to "Expand" phase of product growth.

### 33. Reduce Utilization
**Pattern:** Need to get more done but already at maximum capacity
**Transformation:** What would happen if we *reduced* utilization?
**Detail:** Based on Kingman's Formula. Counterintuitive but can improve flow.

### 34. Self-description
**Pattern:** Using one Thinkie to explain another
**Transformation:** Step back and think "If this were an X, then logical consequences are Y & Z"
**Detail:** Classified under "models" or "analogy."

### 35. Separate Observation From Judgement
**Pattern:** Getting carried away in reaction to situation
**Transformation:** List externally observable facts first. Then judge with list in hand
**Example:** Receiving negative feedback from boss—list facts to avoid defensiveness.

### 36. Special-Cause Versus Common-Cause Variation
**Pattern:** Process has gone haywire for some reason
**Transformation:** Decide if reason happens repeatedly (common cause) or just this once (special cause). React accordingly
**Detail:** Process control Thinkie from Deming.

### 37. Tradeoff
**Pattern:** Someone trying to maximize outcome, frustrated that progress has slowed
**Transformation:** Ask "How much increase do you need? What are you giving up? What will increase friction?"
**Detail:** Has dedicated "Tradeoff Booklet" series.

### 38. Upside/Downside
**Pattern:** Faced with evenly-balanced decision
**Transformation:** List upside and downside consequences. If upside and no downside, proceed
**Detail:** Helps when overwhelmed by details during decision-making.

### 39. Work/Wait Ratio
**Pattern:** Multi-step process where responsibility moves from person to person
**Transformation:** Trace typical work item. Add up work time vs wait time. Divide these totals
**Detail:** Calculation alone can transform process by revealing inefficiencies.

### 40. Agreements
**Pattern:** Locked in argument, want progress
**Transformation:** Pause and list everything you agree on
**Detail:** "Almost like self-help or inter-personal advice." From Jay Bazuzi.

### 41. Analogy
**Pattern:** Struggling to explain a concept
**Transformation:** Explain it in terms of another domain your listener already understands
**Detail:** Analogizing gets easier with practice.

### 42. And Then…
**Pattern:** You have potential decision to evaluate
**Transformation:** Ask "…and then?"
**Example:** Setting higher goals for lines of code to increase productivity.

### 43. But If You Could...
**Pattern:** Someone stuck trying to make progress. They fantasize about what would be required to get unstuck
**Transformation:** Respond with "But what if you could…"
**Detail:** People often know what to do but prevent themselves from doing it.

### 44. Composed Solution
**Pattern:** Stuck solving big problem
**Transformation:** Imagine sequence of steps that seem like they would solve problem
**Detail:** Contrasts with Scale Down. Solves whole problem by seeing it as pipeline.

### 45. Easy Bit
**Pattern:** Paralyzed by magnitude of problem facing you
**Transformation:** Pick absolutely easiest thing to do that will help and do it
**Detail:** Confidence and energy are more important than ideas.

### 46. Explore or Extract? (Explore/Exploit)
**Pattern:** Doing something for nth time. Do it predictably good way or new way?
**Transformation:** Mentally flip a coin so you don't feel bad about either option
**Detail:** Not strictly 50/50—adjust odds based on situation.

### 47. Exponential Growth / Growth Rate, Not Absolute Number
**Pattern:** Is this small thing competition for this large thing?
**Transformation:** Compare growth rates, not absolute size
**Example:** Early COVID—people compared deaths to traffic fatalities, ignored exponential growth rate.

### 48. Extrapolate Further
**Pattern:** Trying to predict consequences of trend
**Transformation:** Extrapolate further than other people, further than seems reasonable
**Detail:** From watching Bill Joy talk about processor performance.

### 49. Fun Bit
**Pattern:** Paralyzed by size and complexity of problem
**Transformation:** Pick the bit that will be most fun and do that
**Detail:** Confidence/energy management strategy.

### 50. Hard Problem
**Pattern:** Stuck addressing hairy problem, or have nagging feeling you're missing something important
**Transformation:** List absolutely everything you can think of that makes the problem hard
**Detail:** One of few Thinkies that generate ideas when faced with intimidating problem.

### 51. Hill Climbing
**Pattern:** Something isn't good enough but stuck improving it
**Transformation:** Recast as hill climbing—identify fitness function. What means "better"?
**Detail:** One of Beck's favorite analogies.

### 52. Invert the Goal
**Pattern:** Stuck trying to make progress toward goal
**Transformation:** Try to achieve the opposite
**Example:** Optimizing UX but users won't cooperate—ask "What's largest change resulting in no change in user behavior?"

### 53. Legibility
**Pattern:** Someone estimating or counting something that doesn't matter
**Transformation:** Understand need they're satisfying with those metrics, find way to meet need another way
**Detail:** "False precision creates false control."

### 54. More General Solution
**Pattern:** Stuck solving complex problem
**Transformation:** Solve more general problem of which specific problem is special case
**Detail:** Basic idea comes from mathematics.

### 55. My Perspective
**Pattern:** Having trouble communicating with someone
**Transformation:** Invite them to look at situation from your perspective
**Detail:** Opposite of usual advice. Generates ideas, not necessarily good ones.

### 56. Naive Solution
**Pattern:** Team stuck solving complicated problem, focused on complicated solutions
**Transformation:** Ask "Why is this any more complicated than…"
**Detail:** Works by helping maintain perspective while others stuck in local maximum.

### 57. Nash Equilibrium
**Pattern:** Two or more parties stuck in dysfunctional relationship but everyone refuses to change
**Transformation:** Step back and show whole dynamic to all parties
**Detail:** Fraught because people don't make rational choices.

### 58. One Pass Quickly, One Pass Good
**Pattern:** Software project needing to deliver variations
**Transformation:** Options include delivering all with buffer, or completing subset per iteration
**Detail:** Balances speed with quality.

### 59. Perfect Setup
**Pattern:** Having trouble making progress in situation
**Transformation:** List properties that would set situation up perfectly for success. Back off only reluctantly
**Detail:** Reason for lack of progress often isn't effort, but context.

### 60. Prisoners' Dilemma
**Pattern:** Two or more parties struggling to cooperate
**Transformation:** Model as Prisoners' Dilemma. If fits, apply Put Skin In The Game or iteration
**Detail:** Classic game theory model.

### 61. Put Skin In The Game
**Pattern:** Incentives misaligned or personal experience with outcomes lacking
**Transformation:** Align personal consequences with product or project outcomes
**Detail:** Absence can lead to subtracting value for users.

### 62. Sampling Rate
**Pattern:** Monitoring system and feel like missing something
**Transformation:** Increase your sampling rate
**Detail:** Tradeoff between cost of observation and accuracy.

### 63. Scale Down
**Pattern:** Stuck and overwhelmed trying to solve big problem
**Transformation:** What thing would, if you had it, make big problem more tractable? Make that
**Detail:** Reminder it's okay to solve part of problem.

### 64. Search Space
**Pattern:** Need to act in context where can't predict results of actions
**Transformation:** Treat choice of action as moves around search space. Characterize space. Ensure actions reversible
**Detail:** Identify dimensions of variability.

### 65. Sensitivity Analysis
**Pattern:** Need to tune operation of system with many inputs. Which dial to twist?
**Transformation:** Pick input that seems to matter. Vary it. See if output changes. If not, let it go
**Detail:** Example: motivation for writing Tidy Together?

### 66. Survivable?
**Pattern:** Stuck making decision
**Transformation:** Ask if negative outcome is survivable. If survivable, do quickest, cheapest thing that generates value
**Detail:** From Luca Delanna's book Ergodicity.

### 67. The Real Question
**Pattern:** Someone asks for information that makes no sense
**Transformation:** Ask "What's the real question?"
**Detail:** From Fred George. Gets to actual issue.

### 68. Win/Win
**Pattern:** Stuck on decision because someone will be negatively affected
**Transformation:** What are solutions that would be good for everyone?
**Detail:** Cliché but works. Easy to forget under stress.

### 69. Work Backwards
**Pattern:** Stuck trying to work forwards from where you are towards goal
**Transformation:** Instead, start at goal and work backwards
**Detail:** Essence of TDD and User Stories.

### 70. Your Position
**Pattern:** Conversation gotten stuck short of resolution but conversation remains promising
**Transformation:** Summarize other party's position in way they would agree with
**Detail:** Natural to judge during conversation, but might be missing something.

### 71. Inhibiting Loop
**Pattern:** Want to improve but instead going backwards
**Transformation:** Identify inhibiting loop in system
**Detail:** First in series on systems thinking.

### 72. Aligning Incentive
**Pattern:** Folks working at cross purposes
**Transformation:** Give them incentive that will influence actions towards common goal
**Detail:** Money powerful but tends to distort behavior.

### 73. Perverse Incentive
**Pattern:** People working to sabotage progress towards desired outcome
**Transformation:** Look at their incentives from their perspective. Change incentives. Behavior will follow
**Example:** Sales people poaching sales from other regions.

### 74. Reverse Causality
**Pattern:** Tried to make change by moving upstream
**Transformation:** Treat effect as cause
**Example:** Too many meetings prevent production pushes. Eliminating meetings causes them to spring back up.

### 75. Change Tradeoff Curves
**Pattern:** Stalled trying to make progress changing outcome of tradeoff
**Transformation:** Rather than changing outcome directly, change shape of tradeoff curves
**Example:** Continuous deployment—desire to deploy more frequently hindered by cost per deployment.

### 76. No Time Axis
**Pattern:** Stuck trying to understand or explain relationship between two factors over time
**Transformation:** Ignore time and graph two factors as two axes
**Detail:** Introduces visualizations as major part of thinking strategy.

### 77. Set-Based Design
**Pattern:** Choosing between 2+ options, don't have information needed to choose wisely yet
**Transformation:** Implement some of them all until one is clearly superior
**Detail:** Also called "¿Por qué no los dos?" (Why not both?)

### 78. Reinforcing Loop
**Pattern:** Situation where worse it gets, worse it seems to get. OR Looking for way to create large effects with small investment
**Transformation:** Find feedback loop with even number of negative connections. Work backwards to find intervention point
**Detail:** Addresses vicious cycles where negative conditions compound.

### 79. Influence Diagram
**Pattern:** Big, complicated system acting up and you'd like to regain influence
**Transformation:** Diagram effects you observe in system and their influence on each other
**Detail:** Follows from Separate Observation From Judgement.

### 80. Edges
**Pattern:** Aren't getting enough resources coming in externally to be successful
**Transformation:** Increase interaction of different kinds of resources
**Detail:** Uses pond analogy—irregular edges have strategic advantages over round ones.

### 81. Ask the Genie
**Pattern:** Question you've abandoned as too difficult or time-consuming
**Transformation:** Pose the question to AI system
**Detail:** New habit, highly profitable but variable. Sometimes excellent insights, sometimes distraction.
